Scope threshold

When the problem is no longer just the website

Not every operational issue requires a platform build. Understanding which type of problem you have prevents overbuilding and underbuilding equally.

Level 1

Website problem

Weak credibility, outdated positioning, unclear messaging, poor content structure, low conversion support.

Path: Static Business Site or Business Launch Site or Authority Website
Level 2

Workflow problem

Repeated manual follow-up, scattered information, duplicated work, handoff dependent on individual memory, poor visibility into status.

Path: Discovery Sprint then Platform Starter
Level 3

Systems problem

Recurring friction across departments at scale, operational delays from tool fragmentation, work too dependent on individual knowledge, admin handling affecting growth.

Path: Discovery Sprint then Platform Starter or Ecosystem Build

FAQ

Scope clarity

Do you work with existing WordPress, Webflow, or Wix sites?

No. We build new — we do not modify, restyle, or patch existing installations on WordPress, Webflow, Wix, Squarespace, or similar platforms. If you have an existing site that needs improvement, the path is a Website Audit to understand the gap, or a Discovery Sprint to scope a migration or new build. Attempting to fix a legacy platform installation without understanding root cause is how projects drift.

What do we need to provide before you start?

Written copy for every page (or add the copywriting service), brand guidelines if you have them (colors, fonts, tone), existing logo files if you already have a logo, and photography and video or written confirmation that stock assets are acceptable. We handle logo design, business signs and cards, QR code, and domain registration — these are included in every build package. The project clock starts when all inputs are in our hands — not when you sign the contract. If content arrives late, the timeline extends proportionally.

What is a CMS?

Content Management System — the editing interface used to update site content after launch. We set up the CMS so your team can make changes without developer help.

What does responsive mean?

The site works well on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices. Content reflows, touch targets adjust, and navigation adapts to each screen size.

Is bilingual included?

Bilingual-ready structure is part of our methodology. Full EN/ES content parity is a quoted add-on at +25% of base package — contact us for details. All bilingual content is transcreated — meaning adapted for meaning and tone in each language — not machine-translated.

When should we start with Discovery?

Start with Discovery when you have unclear scope, multiple decision-makers, unclear platform/tool needs, messy legacy situations, or higher-risk approvals. Discovery protects against rework and mis-scoping by defining requirements before build commitment.

What is quoted separately?

Bilingual delivery, copywriting, migrations, ecommerce, advanced analytics, CRM automation, booking systems, calculators, custom integrations, rush delivery, and legacy cleanup are all quoted separately based on project-specific requirements.

What happens after launch?

Launch is not the end. Websites and systems require updates, refinements, and controlled iteration. Support Plans provide post-launch stability, growth enhancements, or embedded ongoing support—depending on your need state. Terms begin with a 3-month minimum and move to month-to-month after that.

When does a website project become a systems project?

A website project addresses visibility and conversion: credibility, messaging, content structure. When the problem involves repeated manual workflows, scattered information, duplicated work, or operational friction that affects growth—this crosses into workflow/systems territory. Use the Website vs Systems guide in this page to identify your level.
