Best Starting Points

Fast ways to orient yourself

For Retirees

Retirement decisions come down to healthcare quality, daily ease, and manageable bureaucracy

  • Start with healthcare depth and routine livability, not nightlife or internet hype.
  • Ask whether you want a denser social city, a calmer coffee-region pace, or coastal humidity.
  • Prefer buildings with straightforward maintenance histories over heroic renovation stories.
  • Use residency guidance and fee checks early so timing stays realistic.

For First-Time Visitors

Tourist charm and long-stay fit are not always the same thing

  • Visit morning, afternoon, and evening before deciding a neighborhood matches your rhythm.
  • Ask how much time you want to spend in cars, on hills, or around humidity and heat.
  • Check whether your likely daily loop is walkable: coffee, groceries, clinic, and social life.
  • Use CasaClaro's city pages to separate vacation energy from full-time living reality.

    Visa Pathways

    Three routes most long-stay residents use

    M-11 Pensionado — Retiree Visa

    • Income requirement: ~$900 USD/month pension (threshold tied to Colombia minimum wage)
    • Validity: 3 years, renewable
    • Path to permanent residency: 5 years continuous
    • Dependents: Spouse and qualifying children can be included

    M-10 Inversionista — Investor Visa

    • Investment threshold: Approximately 100× Colombia minimum wage in qualifying real property or business
    • Real estate: Property purchase value counts toward the threshold
    • Validity: 3 years, renewable; path to permanent residency available

    Digital Nomad / Remote Worker

    • Income requirement: Minimum 3× Colombia minimum wage from remote or foreign-source income
    • Validity: 2 years
    • Restriction: No Colombian-source income permitted under this category

    Thresholds change when Colombia updates its minimum wage. Verify current amounts at Cancillería de Colombia or Migración Colombia before filing.

    Before Arrival

    Document checklist

    • Apostille your birth certificate through your home country’s designated authority
    • Obtain an FBI background check (or national equivalent) and apostille it
    • Notify Social Security or your pension provider of your foreign address plan
    • Confirm your health insurance covers Colombia or arrange private prepaid coverage before arrival

    Common Mistakes

    What slows people down

    • Buying property before confirming which visa category your situation actually qualifies for
    • Using 2021–2023 blog posts to verify thresholds — category names and amounts have changed since
    • Treating fee screenshots as permanent when official schedules update regularly
    • Skipping independent legal counsel for foreign investment registration on the investor path

    First 90 Days

    • Validate healthcare and insurance strategy before you assume local coverage works the same way it does at home.
    • Rent before you buy if climate, altitude, or neighborhood rhythm are still unknowns.
    • Walk the area morning and evening. A city can be charming at noon and exhausting by night.
    • Ask directly about repairs, backup power, water pressure, and building reserves.
