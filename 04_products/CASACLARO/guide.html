Beginner's Guide

Buying property in Colombia, step by step

A plain-language starting point for investors, relocators, retirees, and first-time Colombia buyers who need the full picture before any single listing frames the decision.

  1. Explore cities first. Use the city explorer and map before you lock onto a neighborhood or unit — cities have more variance than listing photos suggest.
  2. Check relocation fit. If this may become your home, open the relocation page and compare healthcare, walkability, repairs, and retiree fit.
  3. Review residency early. Use the residency page to see whether your investor or pension strategy lines up with current official guidance.
  4. Model real costs. Use the simulator for closing costs, renovation reserve, and total monthly cost — including HOA, maintenance, and any loan service.
  5. Line up professionals. CasaClaro is structured for agents, brokers, lawyers, contractors, and property managers, not just sales agents.
  6. Do title and building diligence. Your lawyer should verify title, liens, HOA rules, foreign investment registration, and use restrictions before funds are released.
  7. Underwrite repairs honestly. Coastal properties, older towers, and low-priced units often look cheap because maintenance reality has not been priced correctly.
  8. Plan the first 30 days. Each city guide includes a first-month setup checklist specific to that market — banking, insurance, SIM, healthcare, and residency filing sequence.
