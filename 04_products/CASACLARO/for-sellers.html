For Sellers

List a home with pricing that reflects what buyers will actually ask

Give buyers the neighborhood facts, repair details, honest pricing, and timing clarity they will ask for anyway — before the first inquiry, not after.

Before you list

  • Collect title, tax, HOA, and occupancy information before a buyer asks for it.
  • Write down known repairs instead of waiting for them to emerge during due diligence.
  • Be honest about whether the property is turnkey, lightly dated, or heading for a heavier renovation.
  • For coastal homes, assume buyers will ask about corrosion, waterproofing, generators, and reserve quality.

What happens after you submit

  • CasaClaro reviews fit, market, price framing, and documentation gaps within one business day.
  • If the home fits the current network, it can be routed to a local agent, broker, or buyer flow.
  • Attachments and notes are already structured for future CRM and upload endpoints.

Repairs Matter

Why repair disclosure matters

Repair reality is part of the marketing story whether sellers mention it or not. A calm, clear repair narrative helps with pricing, buyer matching, and closing certainty.

  • Coastal wear should be documented early.
  • Older urban buildings need HOA reserve position and systems history documented.
  • Recent upgrades should be listed clearly so buyers do not assume the worst.
  • Deferred maintenance almost always becomes a pricing conversation, even if it starts as an inspection issue.

When Uploads Go Live

  • Prioritize tax receipts, HOA statements, title documents, and clear photos of the highest-risk repair items.
  • Include inspection summaries or contractor quotes if you already have them.
  • Use the notes box for occupancy, rental history, or closing constraints that affect timing and buyer fit.
