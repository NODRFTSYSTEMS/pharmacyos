# Walcott & Co. Press — Revised Homepage Layout Blueprint (Handoff Version)

## Governing Source
This blueprint is governed **only** by the uploaded **Walcott Master Business Plan V1**.

### Non-Negotiable Rules from the Business Plan
- The direct platform must function as a **storefront, lead-capture system, content hub, and conversion asset**.
- The website cannot operate as a brochure-only site.
- The homepage must deliver **positioning, featured offers, proof, and lead capture**.
- Launch should be **narrow in operation**, with **limited first offers** and **direct audience building from day one**.
- Books and publications are a core asset stack and should support direct product pages, marketplace listings, email sequences, media content, and promotional assets.
- Shop is a controlled expansion layer and should not overtake the core publishing and Studio message.

---

## Project Context
Walcott has approximately **10–15 in-house manuscripts** and **4 house authors**. These are not only publications; they are proof assets. They should be used to:
- demonstrate Walcott’s publishing standards,
- show that the internal process is being tested on proprietary titles,
- build audience and preorder interest before release,
- create visible market proof for future Studio work.

This materially changes the homepage architecture.

The homepage must now do two things at once, in the correct order:
1. Sell Walcott’s **pre-launch visibility and publishing infrastructure** services.
2. Prove Walcott’s credibility through **its own publications and house authors**.

The second supports the first. It must not overpower it.

---

## Primary Objective
Make the visitor understand, within the first five seconds, that Walcott solves the real problem most serious books face:

**books reach the market before demand, audience, and digital structure have been built.**

The homepage must make authors, publishers, and expert-led book projects feel:
- understood,
- correctly diagnosed,
- commercially protected,
- and guided by a more structured system than the market usually offers.

---

## Primary Audiences
### Primary
- Authors with serious manuscripts who need launch infrastructure.
- Small publishers that need execution capacity, visibility support, and digital systems.
- Experts and entrepreneurs turning expertise into books or authority assets.

### Secondary
- Readers discovering Walcott’s own catalog.
- Media, reviewers, institutions, or trade contacts exploring forthcoming titles.

---

## Core Positioning
Walcott is not to be framed as a generic marketing agency, generic AI content firm, or narrow freelance service shop.

The public homepage position should be:

**Walcott builds the publishing infrastructure that allows books to enter the market with audience, visibility, and conversion systems already in place.**

This includes:
- digital architecture,
- title and author websites,
- launch content,
- audience capture systems,
- publishing support,
- and Walcott’s own publication program as internal proof.

---

## Homepage Job Hierarchy
The homepage has one primary job and three secondary jobs.

### Primary Job
Generate qualified inquiries and email capture for Walcott’s core service offers.

### Secondary Jobs
1. Establish institutional credibility.
2. Showcase Walcott’s own books and forthcoming titles.
3. Move visitors into deeper pages: Studio, Publications, Authors, Insights, Contact.

### What the Homepage Is **Not**
- not a full ecosystem index,
- not a complete catalog,
- not a Shop landing page,
- not a passive brand brochure.

---

## Recommended Public Navigation (Version 1)
Use the following main navigation for clarity:
- Home
- Publications
- Authors
- Studio
- Insights
- About
- Contact
- Book a Strategy Call

### Navigation Notes
- Replace **Publish** with **Publications** in public-facing navigation. “Publications” is clearer, more legible, and more obviously catalog-oriented.
- Do **not** place **Vault** in the primary nav until it is live and explained.
- Do **not** place **Audio** in the primary nav until it has a real destination and offer logic.
- Do **not** place **Shop** in the primary nav for version 1 unless it is active, stocked, and strategically important.
- **Submit** should not be top-level unless submissions are open and operationally ready.

---

## Primary Conversion Paths
The homepage must support **two direct conversion systems**, not one:

### Conversion Path A — Service Inquiry
For authors, publishers, and expert-led projects needing Walcott’s infrastructure.

Primary actions:
- Book a Strategy Call
- Request a Project Review
- Contact the Studio

### Conversion Path B — Publication Interest / Sales
For Walcott’s own titles and forthcoming books.

Primary actions:
- Buy on Amazon
- Order via retailer / bookstore
- Get notified at release
- Join the title launch list
- Bulk / institutional inquiry

These are distinct actions and must not be collapsed into one generic CTA section.

---

1. Hero / Above the Fold
2. Trust and Qualification Strip
3. Problem Diagnosis
4. Core Studio Offers
5. How Walcott Works
6. Proof of Internal Practice (Founder / Process / Publishing Proof)
7. Featured Publications
8. Coming Soon / Forthcoming Titles
9. House Authors
10. Lead Magnet / Email Capture
11. Insights Preview
12. Segmented Engagement Paths
13. Final CTA
14. Footer

### Notes on Order
- **Publications** should appear on the homepage, but **after** service positioning and proof.
- **Coming Soon** must exist as a conversion mechanism, not a decorative label.
- **Email capture** must appear before the final CTA, not only as an implied future function.
- **Shop** is omitted from the homepage spine in version 1.

# Section-by-Section Blueprint

## 1. Hero / Above the Fold

### Goal
Communicate the core problem and Walcott’s value in under five seconds.

### Recommended Headline
**Books should not reach the market before demand exists.**

### Alternate Strong Option
**We build the audience, visibility, and digital structure a book needs before release.**

### Avoid
- “Most books are published too late to matter.”
- Overclaims, absolutes, or emotionally inflated phrasing.

### Recommended Subheading
We design the websites, launch content, and audience systems that help authors, publishers, and expert-led book projects build visibility, capture interest, and create demand before publication.

### CTA Structure
Primary CTA: **Book a Strategy Call**
Secondary CTA: **See How Walcott Works**
Tertiary micro-link: **Explore Publications**

### Hero Support Line
Built for authors, publishers, and book projects that need serious launch infrastructure.

### Hero Visual Requirement
The hero must include a real visual asset, not a decorative letterform alone.

Recommended visual:
- editorial dashboard mockup,
- author/title website panels,
- short-form content previews,
- lead capture module,
- publication page or preorder interface.

The visitor must be able to see what Walcott actually builds.

---

## 2. Trust and Qualification Strip

### Goal
Reinforce authority immediately after the hero.

### Use factual, non-hyped signals
Recommended examples:
- Publishing infrastructure for books and authority-led projects
- Title pages, launch systems, and audience capture
- Internal publication program across house authors and forthcoming works
- Structured for conversion, not just presentation

### Do Not Use
Claims that sound like proof but are merely adjectives.

If possible, replace generic copy with trust objects such as:
- number of in-house manuscripts in development,
- house authors,
- “Available now” and “Coming soon” publication counts,
- active channels or content system categories.

---

## 3. Problem Diagnosis

### Goal
Name the failures Walcott solves.

### Recommended Headline
**Most books begin visibility work too late.**

### Supporting Intro
By the time many authors and publishers think about websites, content, launch strategy, or audience capture, the release window is already weaker than it should be. The result is predictable: fragmented messaging, low preorder momentum, weak direct audience ownership, and overdependence on marketplaces alone.

### Recommended Cards
#### No Audience Before Release
Many books reach the market before any demand has been deliberately built around them.

#### Scattered Digital Presence
Websites, social channels, launch assets, and title pages often exist in fragments instead of operating as one system.

#### No Conversion Path
Attention without inquiry flow, email capture, preorder logic, or title-page conversion becomes noise rather than revenue.

---

## 4. Core Studio Offers

### Goal
Present Walcott’s services in concrete, commercially legible terms.

### Rule
Use offer architecture that aligns with the business plan rather than generic agency categories.

### Recommended Core Homepage Offers
#### Book Launch Sprint
For authors and publishers who need launch structure before release.

#### Authority Site Sprint
For books, experts, and author brands that need a credible digital home.

#### Content Engine Setup
For projects that need article, blog, newsletter, and audience pathways tied to book visibility.

#### Publisher Support Desk
For small presses that need execution support across multiple titles.

### Optional Fifth Offer
#### Author Visibility System
Use only if this offer is being actively sold and scoped.

### Required Card Structure
Each service block should answer:
- who it is for,
- what problem it solves,
- what it includes,
- why it matters commercially.

### Tone Rule
No generic “creative services” language.
The language should feel structural, operational, and outcome-aware.

---

## 5. How Walcott Works

### Goal
Provide process confidence.

### Recommended 4-Step Sequence
1. Diagnose the launch gap
2. Build the digital foundation
3. Produce the launch assets
4. Convert attention into inquiry, capture, preorder, or sale

### Process Tone
This section should feel methodical, not inspirational.

### Rule
Keep this section distinct from “Studio.”
It is a process explanation, not the Studio landing page.

---

## 6. Proof of Internal Practice

### Goal
Replace weak placeholder testimonials with stronger institutional proof.

### Recommended Formats
Use one or more of these instead of representative testimonials:

#### Founder Note
A short statement explaining that Walcott is testing and refining its methods on its own catalog before extending them outward.

#### Internal Publishing Proof
Examples:
- 10–15 manuscripts in development
- 4 house authors
- forthcoming title pipeline
- active publication program

#### Process Proof
Examples:
- title page development
- launch content system
- direct audience capture
- preorder readiness
- POD distribution setup

### Rule
Do not publish stylized placeholder testimonials on the live site.
If no real client feedback exists yet, use founder proof and publication proof instead.

---

## 7. Featured Publications

### Goal
Show Walcott’s publications as proof assets and sales assets.

### Section Role
This section is not merely decorative. It proves that Walcott is actively publishing and creating its own catalog while also creating downstream sales opportunities.

### Recommended Headline
**Selected titles from the Walcott catalog**

### Recommended Content
Feature 3–6 titles only.
Each title card should include:
- cover image,
- title,
- category or imprint,
- one-sentence positioning line,
- status: Available Now / Coming Soon,
- CTA.

### CTA Logic for Available Titles
- Buy on Amazon
- View Title Page
- Bulk / Institutional Inquiry

### CTA Logic for Coming Soon Titles
- View Title Page
- Join the Release List
- Get Notified at Launch

### Grid Rule
Do not place a catalog-navigation card inside the same visual grid as actual book cards.
A “View All Publications” link should be below the grid or visually distinct.

---

## 8. Coming Soon / Forthcoming Titles

### Goal
Turn future books into list-building and proof-building assets.

### Strategic Role
A “Coming Soon” label alone is weak. Forthcoming titles should capture email interest and validate audience response before release.

### Recommended Section Headline
**Forthcoming titles**

### Recommended Content Per Title
- title,
- author,
- short premise,
- expected release window,
- CTA: Join the Title List.

### Required CTA Options
- Get release notification
- Join the launch list
- Request review copy interest (if relevant)

### Why This Matters
This section proves pipeline depth and creates pre-release audience capture around Walcott’s own IP.

---

## 9. House Authors

### Goal
Humanize the catalog and make Walcott feel like a real publishing institution, not only a service interface.

### Recommended Section Headline
**House authors**

### Recommended Structure
Feature the 4 house authors with:
- author portrait or placeholder,
- name,
- discipline or lane,
- short positioning line,
- linked titles,
- CTA: View Author.

### Strategic Benefit
This supports both:
- publication credibility,
- and service credibility.

It signals that Walcott understands authorship from the inside, not only as a vendor.

Satisfy the business plan’s requirement for lead capture and owned audience building.

### Recommended Lead Magnet Options

#### For Publications

Use one email capture block with two clear entry paths:
- **For authors and publishers**
- **For readers and title followers**

### Rule
Do not rely on the final CTA alone. The homepage must contain at least one true audience capture device.

## 12. Segmented Engagement Paths

### Goal
Help visitors self-identify without sending them all to the same generic section.

### Recommended Paths

#### I need an author or title website

#### I want to explore Walcott publications

Segmented cards must not all point to the same anchor. Each must route to a relevant destination.

Walcott helps authors, publishers, and expert-led projects build the digital structure required for visibility, audience capture, and conversion before release.

### CTA Options
Primary: **Book a Strategy Call**

- booking page,
- contact form,

# Copy Direction

## Voice
- precise,
- controlled,
- intelligent,
- commercially literate,
- unsentimental,
- non-generic.

## Preferred Language
Use words like:
- visibility,
- launch,
- audience,
- structure,
- system,
- inquiry,
- demand,
- title page,
- direct audience,
- publishing infrastructure.

## Avoid
- innovative,
- tailored,
- impactful,
- transformative,
- comprehensive,
- curated,
- end-to-end,
- vague “everything changes” phrasing.

## Tone Consistency Rules
- Use first-person voice (“we”), not intermittent third-person brand narration.
- Keep audience labels consistent.
- Avoid introducing “brands” unless explicitly defined.
- Keep B2B publishing infrastructure tone dominant on the homepage.
