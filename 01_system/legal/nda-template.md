(a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;

(c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure;
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or
(e) is required to be disclosed by applicable law, regulation, or court order, provided the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice (where legally permissible) and cooperates with the Disclosing Party to limit the scope of the required disclosure.
