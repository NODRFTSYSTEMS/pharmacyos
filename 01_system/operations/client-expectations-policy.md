# Client Expectations Policy
# Classification: Internal — Proprietary
# Version: 1.0 | 2026-04-24

## Purpose

Define the NoDrftSystems position on six known client expectation gaps. This policy governs what must be stated in every SOW, intake confirmation, and client onboarding communication — so ambiguity is resolved before work begins, not during it.

---

## 1. Content Delivery Definition

**The gap:** "Client supplies all content" is stated in the tier table but not defined. Clients may deliver incomplete, unformatted, or incorrect content and believe they have met the requirement.

**Policy:**
- Content delivery is complete when the client has provided:
  - All written copy in final-draft form (not notes, outlines, or "ideas")
  - Logo files in SVG and PNG (minimum 1000px wide), with brand color hex codes
  - All images or approved image sources (rights confirmed by client)
  - Any legal disclaimers, regulatory language, or compliance-mandated copy
- Partial content delivery does not start the timeline clock for that section of the build
- Acceptable format: Google Docs, Word, or plain text — not PDF images, photographs of documents, or embedded in email
- NoDrftSystems will provide a Content Delivery Checklist to the client at project kickoff. The checklist is the authoritative reference for what "content delivered" means for that engagement.

**What goes in the SOW:** "Project timeline begins when all items on the Content Delivery Checklist are received and confirmed complete by NoDrftSystems."

---

## 2. Timeline Hold Policy

**The gap:** What happens when a client delivers content late is not defined. This creates disputes about blame, restart timing, and any expedite fees.

**Policy:**
- The project timeline clock starts on the date NoDrftSystems confirms the Content Delivery Checklist is complete — not on the date the SOW is signed.
- If the client does not deliver content within [X business days] of the project start date (defined per SOW), the project enters HOLD status.
- In HOLD status: no build work proceeds; the scheduled delivery date shifts by the number of business days in HOLD plus [X] business days buffer for workload re-scheduling.
- If HOLD extends beyond [Y business days], the engagement requires a Change Order to reactivate with a revised timeline and, at NoDrftSystems' discretion, a re-scheduling fee.
- [X] and [Y] are engagement-specific and must be defined in the SOW. This policy sets the framework; the SOW sets the specific numbers.

**What goes in the SOW:** "Delivery timeline begins on content confirmation date. Late delivery triggers HOLD status with timeline shift as defined above."

## 3. Bug vs. Feature Definition

**The gap:** The SOW states "post-launch bugs covered per support window" but does not define what a bug is. Clients frequently frame feature requests as bugs.

**Policy:**
A **bug** is behavior that:
- Was explicitly scoped in the SOW acceptance criteria AND
- Is not working as specified

A **feature** is:
- Any behavior not specified in the SOW
- Any change to approved design or functionality
- Any expansion of content or sections beyond what was delivered

**Examples that are bugs:** A form submit button does not send data. A page does not load on mobile. A link goes to a 404.
**Examples that are features:** "Can we add a new section?" "Can we change the color scheme?" "Can we add a chat widget?"

All feature requests during the support window require a Change Order. Disputed classifications are resolved by NoDrftSystems' written determination, with Founder as final authority.

**What goes in the SOW:** "Post-launch support covers defects in delivered functionality as scoped. New functionality, design changes, and scope additions require a Change Order."

## 4. SLA Tier Visibility Pre-SOW

**The gap:** Response SLA tiers (standard / priority / emergency) are defined in the SOW but not visible during the sales process. Clients choosing between vendors may not know NoDrftSystems' response commitments.

**Policy:**
- SLA tiers must be visible in the service package description before the SOW is signed — not only inside the SOW.
- The three tiers and their response windows are:
  - Standard: 48-hour response
  - Priority: 24-hour response
  - Emergency: 4-hour response
- Each tier is priced differently and must appear in the retainer pricing structure
- The default tier is Standard unless Priority or Emergency is explicitly scoped and priced in the SOW
- No SLA commitment is binding unless it is in a signed SOW — verbal SLA commitments are not honored

**What goes in the SOW:** Full SLA tier, response window, and scope of what qualifies as an emergency.

## 5. Discovery Credit Terms

**The gap:** "Discovery fee credited toward next package if contracted within 30 days" is the stated policy, but two ambiguities create disputes: (a) Does the credit apply if the client chooses a different project type than originally discussed? (b) What is the credit amount — the full $2,000 or a portion?

**Policy:**
- The Discovery credit is the full Discovery Sprint fee ($2,000)
- The credit applies when:
  - The client contracts a NoDrftSystems service package within 30 calendar days of the Discovery Sprint delivery date
  - The contracted package is for the same client and business concept evaluated in the Discovery Sprint
- The credit does NOT apply if:
  - The 30-day window has passed (no extensions; this protects capacity allocation)
  - The client contracts a different business concept or a different legal entity than the one evaluated
  - The client requests a second Discovery Sprint (each Discovery Sprint is a separate engagement)
- The credit applies to any package tier (T1–T5) or retainer — it is not restricted to the package discussed during Discovery
- The credited amount reduces the project invoice; it is not a cash refund

**What goes in the SOW / Discovery delivery:** Credit terms stated in writing at Discovery delivery. 30-day clock starts on delivery date, not signing date.

## 6. T1 vs. T1S Client-Facing Differentiation

**The gap:** T1 (Conversion Landing Page Sprint) and T1S (Static Business Site) sound similar to clients. The value differentiation is not immediately clear from the tier names alone.

**Policy for client-facing language:**

| | T1 — Conversion Landing Page Sprint | T1S — Static Business Site |
|---|---|---|
| Best for | One specific campaign, product launch, or lead capture goal | Full business presence with multiple services or information pages |
| Pages | 1 page, up to 8 sections | Up to 5 pages (Home, About, Services, Contact + 1 optional) |
| CMS | No — static build | No — static build |
| Primary goal | Drive one action (book a call, sign up, buy) | Establish credibility and present the full business |
| Copywriting | Client-supplied | Client-supplied |
| Updates after launch | Change Order required for any content change | Change Order required for any content change |

Client-facing descriptions must use this differentiation language — not the internal tier code names — in all sales and onboarding materials.
