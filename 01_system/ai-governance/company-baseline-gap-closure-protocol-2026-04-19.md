## Objective

Convert company-level unknowns, unresolved public-proof status, and proprietary-surface governance gaps into controlled artifacts with named owners, explicit evidence rules, and mandatory human gates.

## Analysis

- Unknown company facts are not solved by stronger wording. They are solved by evidence, approval, and controlled documentation.
- The correct operating response is to separate:
  - facts already verified
  - facts not verifiable with available data
  - decisions still reserved to the Founder or qualified counsel
  - structural gaps that can be closed immediately through governance work
- Company-level claims require tighter discipline than ordinary internal drafts because they propagate into business plans, public routes, proposals, and legal-adjacent materials.

## Scope

This protocol governs closure work for:

- legal entity specifics
- live cost base, burn, runway, and profitability baselines
- active revenue, MRR, pipeline, and close-rate baselines
- public proof inventory and claim-approval controls
- routine-usage pricing figures and customer-facing pricing posture

## Exclusions

- legal advice
- tax advice
- accounting certification
- investor-positioning claims unsupported by current evidence
- automatic approval of public proof, pricing figures, or legal-entity claims

## Workflow

1. Inventory the claim or structural gap.
2. Classify it as one of: factual unknown, approval missing, evidence missing, or governance-structure gap.
3. Route to the correct operating cell and load the required workflow skill.
4. Create or update the controlling artifact before updating downstream strategy, copy, or release materials.
5. Mark any unresolved item as `Not verifiable with available data.` Do not substitute assumptions.
6. Run independent control review:
   - `reviewer_public_proof` for proof claims
   - `LCA` for legal-adjacent surfaces
   - `QAS` for readiness and consistency
7. Escalate all non-delegable items to the Founder. If ARE routing is unavailable, route directly to the Founder per standing decision authority.
8. Update downstream documents only after the controlling artifact is in place.

## Risks

- false certainty created by repeating unverified business claims
- public proof leakage before approval
- pricing drift if routine-usage figures are implied before a Founder decision
- release drift if a proprietary public route remains active without a paired workspace and control record
- legal exposure if entity or terms claims are published without qualified review

## Acceptance Criteria

- every company-level unknown is either closed with evidence or explicitly marked not verifiable
- each open item has a controlling artifact, owner, and next required decision
- no public proof item is approved unless it is logged in the public proof inventory
- no routine-usage price is treated as operative until the decision brief is resolved
- every active proprietary public-commercial route has a paired `02_client-system/` workspace and control-plane record
- the business plan, control plane, registry, and active workspace records no longer contradict each other on website governance status
