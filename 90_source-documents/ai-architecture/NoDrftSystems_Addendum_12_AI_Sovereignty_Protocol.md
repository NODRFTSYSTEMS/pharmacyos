ADDENDUM 12
AI Sovereignty Protocol Definition


# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The AI Sovereignty Protocol defines how NoDrftSystems maintains control, ownership, and accountability over all AI-generated outputs while delivering exceptional value to clients. This protocol establishes clear boundaries between AI assistance and human judgment, ensuring compliance, quality, and ethical standards.

# 1. CORE PRINCIPLES
Human-in-the-Loop (HITL): All AI-generated outputs require human review, validation, and approval before client delivery. AI augments human capability; it does not replace human judgment.
Accountability: NoDrftSystems maintains full legal and professional accountability for all deliverables, regardless of AI involvement in their creation.
Transparency: Clients are informed of AI involvement as required by contract and applicable law. NoDrftSystems discloses AI usage in accordance with MSA Section 2.2.
Quality Assurance: AI outputs undergo the same rigorous QA process as human-created work, including the Multi-Pass Checklist defined in Addendum 8.
Data Sovereignty: Client data is never used to train third-party AI models without explicit written consent.
Ethical Use: AI is used responsibly and ethically, in compliance with applicable laws and professional standards.

# 3. HUMAN REVIEW REQUIREMENTS
All AI-generated content must undergo human review before client delivery. The review process includes:
Accuracy Verification: Fact-check all claims, data, and references.
Contextual Appropriateness: Ensure content is appropriate for client industry and audience.
Brand Alignment: Verify consistency with client brand voice and guidelines.
Legal Compliance: Check for potential IP, privacy, or regulatory issues.
Technical Correctness: Validate code, calculations, and technical specifications.
Completeness: Confirm all requirements from SOW are addressed.

# 4. CLIENT DATA HANDLING
NoDrftSystems adheres to strict data handling protocols when using AI tools:
Client confidential information is only processed through approved AI tools with appropriate data protection agreements.
No client data is used to train or improve third-party AI models without explicit written consent.
All AI tool providers must have SOC 2 Type II certification or equivalent security standards.
Client data is deleted from AI tool systems after project completion unless retention is contractually required.
Anonymized or synthetic data is used for testing and development where possible.

# 5. APPROVED AI TOOLS
NoDrftSystems maintains a whitelist of approved AI tools. All tools must be reviewed and approved by Operations before use on client projects.

Note: The approved tools list is maintained internally and updated quarterly. Contact Operations for the current whitelist.

# 6. PROHIBITED AI USES
The following uses of AI are strictly prohibited:
Generating content that infringes on third-party intellectual property rights.
Creating deceptive, misleading, or fraudulent content.
Processing protected health information (PHI) without HIPAA-compliant tools and BAA.
Using AI to make decisions that could have legal or significant consequences without human oversight.
Uploading client confidential data to unapproved or consumer-grade AI tools.
Representing AI-generated content as solely human-created without disclosure (where required).
Using AI to generate content that violates applicable laws or professional ethics.

# 7. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
For audit and compliance purposes, the following must be documented:
Which AI tools were used in creating deliverables.
The extent of AI involvement (AI-Assisted, AI-Generated, AI-Enabled).
Who performed human review and approval.
Any known limitations or caveats related to AI-generated content.
Client disclosure provided (if applicable).

# 8. INCIDENT RESPONSE
In the event of an AI-related incident (e.g., data exposure, incorrect output, compliance issue):
1. Immediately cease using the affected AI tool for client work.
2. Notify Operations within 24 hours.
3. Document the incident, including scope and potential impact.
4. Assess whether client notification is required per MSA terms.
5. Implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence.
6. Update protocols and training as needed.

# 9. TRAINING & COMPLIANCE
All NoDrftSystems team members who use AI tools must:
Complete AI Sovereignty Protocol training before using AI on client projects.
Acknowledge understanding of this protocol in writing.
Complete refresher training annually or when protocol is updated.
Stay informed about changes to approved tools and prohibited uses.

# 10. REVIEW & UPDATES
This protocol is reviewed quarterly to ensure it remains current with:
Evolving AI capabilities and risks.
Changes in legal and regulatory requirements.
Updates to approved AI tools and providers.
Lessons learned from incidents or near-misses.
Industry best practices and standards.
