# Copy Principles
## Tone & Voice
Professional but approachable—not stiff corporate speak
Confident but not arrogant—let results speak
Clear over clever—plain language beats jargon
Specific over vague—concrete examples, not abstract claims
## Plain Language Rules
No acronyms dropped cold—explain or avoid
Technical terms must have buyer-safe explanations
Active voice preferred over passive
Short sentences and paragraphs for web readability
One idea per paragraph
## Bilingual Requirements
English/Spanish parity required for all key pages
Transcreation preferred over literal translation where tone matters
CTA strength must be equivalent in both languages
Legal/privacy language requires professional translation review
## What to Avoid
Superlatives without proof ("best," "leading," "top")
Jargon that assumes technical knowledge
Vague promises ("synergy," "innovation," "solutions")
Process reveals that expose internal methods
Client naming without explicit approval
