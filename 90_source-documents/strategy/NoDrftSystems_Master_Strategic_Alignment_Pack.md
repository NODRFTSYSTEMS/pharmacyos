# Business Architecture
NoDrftSystems operates on a three-engine business model that converts positioning and governance requirements into an investor-legible architecture. Each engine serves a distinct purpose in the company's growth trajectory.
## Cash Engine (Now)
The cash engine consists of structured website and digital-build packages with controlled pricing presentation, explicit inclusions/exclusions, and disciplined qualification. This is what buyers can purchase today and what drives immediate revenue.

## Expansion Engine (Next)
The expansion engine encompasses platform/system work and broader ecosystems positioned as an upper-tier progression, not the dominant entry message. This includes complex integrations, custom applications, and multi-surface digital ecosystems for clients who have outgrown standard packages.
## Asset Engine (Moat)
The asset engine consists of governed operating system artifacts that compound over time: package briefs, copy modules, glossary (buyer-safe technical explanations), QA checklists, review passes, completion reports, and release gates. These are internally documented and externally framed as standards-not disclosed as proprietary methods.

# Offer Ladder & Pricing
The website uses a package ladder that helps buyers self-identify by scope, urgency, and business objective. Prices are shown only where explicitly verified and approved.
## Package Integrity Rules
Each package must answer: what it is, who it is for, what is included, what is excluded, and what happens next.
Package names remain stable once approved so pricing, sales, copy, and implementation stay aligned.
Use 'from' only where variability is real and expected. Fixed packages are presented as fixed.
Lower-tier offers do not quietly absorb custom CMS, full bilingual delivery, or complex integrations by default.
Every package includes a clear upgrade path so buyers can see the next logical step.
