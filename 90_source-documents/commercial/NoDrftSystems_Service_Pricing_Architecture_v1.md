# 1. Operating Principles
Every tier in this document is built on the same four commitments. These are not marketing language. They are operational constraints that govern what we will and will not take on at each level.

### Clarity before build
We do not begin design or development until scope is documented and signed off. If scope is unclear, the Discovery Sprint is the required entry point. There are no exceptions to this rule regardless of urgency.
### Defined inclusions and hard exclusions
Every package in this document states what is included and what is not. Exclusions are not negotiable by default. They can be added as documented add-ons with separate pricing. If a client expects something not listed, that expectation must be corrected before contract execution.
### Fixed price, bounded scope
All fixed packages are quoted at a flat fee. The fee does not change unless the scope changes via a signed Change Order. Hourly engagements are the exception and are subject to separate terms.
### Fail-closed release
Nothing ships without passing the QA multi-pass review and receiving a human sign-off. Not under deadline pressure. Not to accommodate a client request for early access. The release gate is non-negotiable.

Hourly engagements are available for consultations, audits, reviews, and short-form tasks where a fixed package is not appropriate or where a client needs expert input before committing to a larger project.

## 3.2 What Hourly Covers

Hourly engagements are advisory or task-specific in nature. The deliverable for a consultation is a written summary note, not a full strategy document. If a client expects a complete deliverable from a 1-hour session, that expectation should be corrected before booking. If the scope of an hourly task exceeds the initial estimate by more than 50%, we will stop and present a revised estimate before continuing.


# 4. Fixed Package Ladder
Fixed packages are the core commercial offer. Each package has a defined scope, a flat fee, a stated timeline, a complete list of inclusions, and a complete list of exclusions. The client knows exactly what they are buying before they sign.

Package pricing reflects a U.S.-market quality standard delivered through a human-led, AI-augmented production model. These are not template prices inflated to look premium. They are engineered to support 75%+ contribution margins while remaining competitive against boutique agencies in the $150–$250/hr range.

For projects where scope is unclear, systems are complex, or the client is evaluating multiple approaches. The Discovery Sprint is a decision-making tool, not a build. No design or code is produced. The output is a bounded scope brief, execution plan, and package recommendation.

Clients who are not sure what they need built

# 6. Retainer Tiers
Retainers are for clients who need ongoing support, iteration, or continuous improvement after a project has launched. They are not a substitute for a new project build. Retainers do not absorb new feature builds; those are scoped and priced separately as Change Orders.


## 6.1 What Retainer Hours Cover
Bug fixes and minor content corrections
Performance monitoring and updates
Dependency and security patches
Minor UX or copy adjustments (under 2 hours per task)
Analytics review and reporting
Third-party integration troubleshooting

## 6.2 What Retainer Hours Do NOT Cover
New pages, new features, or new integrations — these require a Change Order
Redesigns or significant structural changes to existing pages
Content writing or SEO strategy
Work outside the original project's technology stack without prior scoping

## 6.3 Retainer Terms
Retainers are invoiced monthly on the 1st. Work does not begin until payment is received.
After the 3-month minimum, retainers convert to month-to-month with 30-day written notice to cancel.
Unused hours do not carry forward beyond the rollover policy stated above.
Retainer clients receive priority scheduling over new project intake.
A retainer cannot be used to build a new project. New projects require a separate SOW.


# 7. Phased Build Engagement
For projects that exceed the Platform Starter scope but do not require the full Ecosystem Build, or for clients who need to manage budget across multiple phases, we offer structured phased builds.

Each phase is a standalone SOW with its own deliverables, acceptance criteria, and payment schedule. A new SOW must be executed before each subsequent phase begins. A completed phase can be paused without penalty; re-engagement after 90 days requires re-scoping.


## 7.1 Phased Build Rules
Discovery (Phase 0) is mandatory before Phase 1 can be scoped or priced.
Each phase must be accepted and invoiced before the next phase begins.
Phase pricing is estimated at project start. Final pricing per phase is confirmed in the individual SOW.
Changes to Phase 1 output that affect Phase 2 scope will trigger a rescoping session.


# 8. Enterprise Collaboration
Enterprise engagements are custom-scoped projects for organizations with complex requirements, multiple stakeholders, compliance constraints, or systems requiring bespoke architecture. These engagements are not served by the standard package ladder.

Enterprise engagements begin with a paid Discovery engagement (Tier 0 or extended scope) and proceed to a custom Master Service Agreement (MSA) + Statement of Work (SOW) per the NoDrftSystems standard legal framework.

## 8.1 Enterprise Profile
An engagement qualifies as Enterprise when one or more of the following apply:
Project requires more than 3 development specialists across more than 2 disciplines
Timeline exceeds 10 weeks or requires more than 8 weeks of parallel workstreams
Project includes regulated data handling (HIPAA, PCI, SOC 2, or equivalent)
Client requires custom SLA terms, dedicated point of contact, or executive-level reporting
Project involves a custom AI or ML system requiring model training or data pipeline work
Total scope value exceeds $35,000

## 8.2 Enterprise Pricing Structure

## 8.3 Enterprise Engagement Process
Step 1 — Initial call (30 min, no charge): confirm enterprise fit and NDA execution
Step 2 — Discovery engagement: paid scoping producing architecture brief and SOW inputs
Step 3 — Proposal delivery: SOW draft within 5 business days of Discovery completion
Step 4 — MSA + SOW execution: both documents required before any work begins
Step 5 — Kickoff and team assignment: 3–5 days after contract execution
Step 6 — Delivery per phased SOW milestones with documented sign-off at each gate

## 8.4 Enterprise Expectations
Enterprise clients must assign a named internal project lead with authority to approve deliverables. Approval windows are stated in each SOW milestone. If approval is not received within the defined window, the timeline is adjusted forward without penalty to NoDrftSystems. Enterprise projects are not built concurrently with more than one other active project of comparable scope. Capacity must be confirmed at contract execution.

# 11. Payment & General Commercial Terms

## 11.1 Standard Payment Schedule

## 11.2 Payment Rules
All prices are in USD. Payment is accepted via ACH, wire transfer, or approved platform.
Net 10 days from invoice date unless stated otherwise in the SOW.
Late payments accrue interest at 1.5% per month (18% APR) after the due date.
Work is suspended if an invoice remains unpaid 5 business days past due.
Final deliverables, access credentials, and source files are not transferred until full payment is received.
Change Orders are invoiced separately and must be paid before changed scope begins.

## 11.3 Revision Policy
Revisions included per package are defined in each tier. They are used within the defined feedback window only.
A revision is a correction or refinement of what was scoped. A revision is not a new direction, new feature, or conceptual change.
Conceptual changes after design approval are Change Orders, not revisions.
Feedback must be consolidated. Multiple rounds of fragmented feedback do not count as one revision.

## 11.4 Cancellation Policy
Discovery Sprint: non-refundable after the workshop is conducted.
Fixed packages: deposits are non-refundable. Work completed to date is billed against the total at the applicable rate.
Retainers: 30-day written notice after the 3-month minimum. Unused hours in the cancellation month are forfeited.
If NoDrftSystems cancels due to capacity or fit issues before work begins, the full deposit is refunded.


# 12. Client Responsibilities & Expectations
These are not suggestions. They are operating requirements. Projects that do not meet these inputs on schedule will have their timelines adjusted forward. NoDrftSystems does not absorb client-side delays into our delivery schedule.

## 12.1 Before Work Begins
Signed MSA and SOW on file
Deposit payment received and cleared
All brand assets delivered: logo (SVG or high-res), brand colors, existing fonts
All written content delivered in final or near-final form (exceptions noted in SOW)
Named client project lead identified with authority to approve deliverables
Access credentials provided: hosting, domain registrar, existing CMS, analytics, etc.

## 12.2 During the Project
Feedback must be delivered within 48 hours of each deliverable submission at milestone gates
Feedback must be consolidated from all internal stakeholders before submission
Revision requests must reference specific elements and describe the desired change
Client acknowledges that vague feedback ('make it more modern') does not constitute actionable direction
Change requests must be submitted in writing and are subject to Change Order pricing and timeline adjustments

## 12.3 At Launch
Client must confirm final acceptance in writing before the project is released to production
Go-live authorization is a client responsibility, not a default action
Any post-launch issues reported within the support window must be clearly documented with reproduction steps
